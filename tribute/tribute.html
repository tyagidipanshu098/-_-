A Tribute to
Srinivas Ramanujan
1887-1920

Srinivasa Ramanujan Aiyangar was an Indian mathematician who made significant contributions to the field of mathematics, particularly in the area of number theory. Born in 1887 in Erode, Tamil Nadu, India, Ramanujan's mathematical talents were recognized early on, but he faced significant obstacles in pursuing his studies due to his lack of formal education and financial difficulties. Despite these challenges, Ramanujan developed a vast and complex system of mathematical theories and formulas, many of which were well ahead of their time. He is perhaps best known for his work on partition functions and the Ramanujan prime, and his insights have had a profound impact on modern mathematics. Ramanujan passed away in 1920 at the young age of 32, but his legacy continues to inspire mathematicians around the world to this day.
